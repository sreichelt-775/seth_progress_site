Tank-scale tests

Plots and notes for the first ~1900 hours pulled on 2026-04-15.

Update summary

The plots below show the most up-to-date tank dataset through ~1900 hours, pulled on 4/15. After correcting the RH sensor voltage issue and carrying the cleaned RH data forward, I generated the latest RH, heater temperature, soil temperature, and dielectric plots. The overall trends appear to be stabilizing further, particularly at greater distances from the heat source, while the largest changes still remain closest to the heater. I will continue checking the remaining minor spikes and smoothing only where necessary.

RH cleaning and anchored reconstruction

Seen in the first raw-versus-cleaned plots, the initial correction used a rolling-median-based reconstruction to remove the strongest artifacts caused by the unstable power supply and recover the overall trend of the damaged RH and temperature signals.

In the following anchored plots, that first-pass result was refined by visually selecting representative anchor windows from the cleaned record and tying them to the later voltage-regulated data, allowing smooth interpolation across the corrupted interval.

The tail end of the plots, over the last ~50 hours, shows the new voltage-regulated data with minimal noise, indicating that only minor smoothing should be necessary in the future now that the power source is no longer corrupted.

Relative humidity tests

20°C data and next steps.

I fitted the Lu (2016) model to Yu's test data initially by eye and then used a solver for refinement.

Using the parameters determined from the Lu (2016) fit, the nonisothermal SWRC now fits the data realistically. As I obtain more data points from my tests, I will follow the same approach using my updated soil dry density and porosity. I also intend to digitize Yu's higher-temperature data and fit the models similarly in preparation for analyzing my own data later. I will combine everything into a single plot once all the data has been collected, and I will fit the nonisothermal model (2024) to each dataset.
