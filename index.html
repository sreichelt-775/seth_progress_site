Tank-scale tests

Plots and notes for the first ~1900 hours pulled on 2026-04-15.

Update summary

The plots below show the most up-to-date tank dataset through ~1900 hours, pulled on 4/15. After correcting the RH sensor voltage issue and carrying the cleaned RH data forward, I generated the latest RH, heater temperature, soil temperature, and dielectric plots. The RH sensors appear to be stabilizing; however, there are still noticeable changes in the volumetric water content and saturation closest to the heater.

The temperature data from the dielectric sensors show some recent increases and fluctuations across all five locations; however, they are all changing together. This may reflect variability coming from the data-acquisition software itself. The temperature accuracy of these sensors should be within ±0.3°C. Additionally, the temperature recorded at the dielectric sensors located 50 mm from the heaters is reading roughly 8°C higher than that at the RH sensor located at the same radial distance. Given the issues you have had in the past with RH sensors at higher temperatures, I trust the dielectric reading more. This differential could also be related to the fact that the dielectric probes are installed in both granular and pelletized material at different densities, due to their sharp, thin probes being inserted vertically rather than laid horizontally as the RH sensors were.

RH cleaning and anchored reconstruction

Seen in the first raw-versus-cleaned plots, the initial correction used a rolling-median-based reconstruction with provided upper and lower bounds to remove the unrealistic spikes caused by the unstable power supply. This recovered the overall trend of the RH and temperature data prior to the voltage failure and successfully removed the large spikes we discussed previously by email.

In the following anchored plots, that first-pass result was refined by visually selecting representative anchor windows from the cleaned record and tying them to the later voltage-regulated data obtained after fixing the power supply. This allowed smooth interpolation across the smaller spikes and provided a final overall polish that is now tied to the ongoing dataset rather than to the median of the corrupted output voltage. Moving forward, the earlier data will now be tied to the correct trend measured by the RH sensors. I saved the cleaned data separately, and it will be used when stacking future data pulls.

The tail end of the plots, over the last ~50 hours, shows the new voltage-regulated data with minimal noise, indicating that only minor smoothing should be necessary in the future now that the power source is no longer corrupted.

Relative humidity tests

No changes made here. 20°C data and next steps.

I fitted the Lu (2016) model to Yu's test data initially by eye and then used a solver for refinement.

Using the parameters determined from the Lu (2016) fit, the non-isothermal SWRC now fits the data realistically. As I obtain more data points from my tests, I will follow the same approach using my updated soil dry density and porosity. I also intend to digitize Yu's higher-temperature data and fit the models similarly in preparation for analyzing my own data later. I will combine everything into a single plot once all the data has been collected, and I will fit the non-isothermal model (2024) to each dataset.
